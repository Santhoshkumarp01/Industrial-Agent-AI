STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Bearing Replacement — Rolling Mill Drive End Bearing

SOP No: MT-RM-004 | Rev: 3 | Date: 2024-01-15
Equipment: Rolling Mill #1, Rolling Mill #3
Estimated Duration: 4–6 hours

1. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
   1.1 Obtain Permit to Work (PTW) from shift supervisor before starting.
   1.2 Perform Lock Out Tag Out (LOTO) on main drive panel and hydraulic supply.
   1.3 Allow equipment to cool to below 40°C before touching bearing housing.
   1.4 Wear heat-resistant gloves, safety glasses, and steel-toed boots.
   1.5 Ensure fire extinguisher (CO2 type) is available at work site.

2. TOOLS AND MATERIALS REQUIRED
   - Bearing puller (hydraulic, 20-ton capacity)
   - Induction heater (for bearing installation)
   - Torque wrench (calibrated, range 0–200 Nm)
   - Dial indicator with magnetic base
   - SKF LGMT 3 grease, 500g minimum
   - Replacement bearing: SKF-22318 (or SKF-22319 for RM#3 large end)
   - Shaft seal SS-12 (replace whenever bearing is replaced)
   - Cleaning solvent and lint-free rags
   - Digital temperature probe

3. PROCEDURE

   Step 1 — Preparation
   1.1 Confirm LOTO is applied. Test by attempting to start motor from HMI.
   1.2 Drain lubrication from bearing housing into waste oil container.
   1.3 Mark bearing housing alignment with punch marks on housing and shaft.

   Step 2 — Removal
   2.1 Remove bearing housing cover bolts (16 × M20 bolts, 90 Nm torque).
   2.2 Slide out coupling guard and disconnect coupling insert.
   2.3 Attach hydraulic bearing puller to bearing inner race.
   2.4 Apply slow, even pressure. Do not hammer. Bearing should slide off in 5–10 min.
   2.5 Inspect shaft surface for scoring. If scored >0.5mm deep, report to engineer.
   2.6 Clean housing bore with solvent. Remove all old grease and metal particles.

   Step 3 — Installation
   3.1 Heat new bearing in induction heater to 80–100°C (NEVER exceed 120°C).
   3.2 Check temperature with probe before installation.
   3.3 Slide heated bearing onto shaft quickly and evenly. Push fully home.
   3.4 Hold in place for 60 seconds while bearing cools and grips shaft.
   3.5 Apply SKF LGMT 3 grease — fill housing to 1/3 capacity only (not full).
   3.6 Install new shaft seal SS-12. Apply light oil to seal lip before fitting.
   3.7 Refit bearing housing cover. Torque bolts to 90 Nm in cross pattern.

   Step 4 — Post-Installation Checks
   4.1 Rotate shaft by hand — should turn smoothly with no binding or roughness.
   4.2 Check bearing axial play with dial indicator — should be 0.05–0.15 mm.
   4.3 Remove LOTO only after all tools are cleared and guards are fitted.
   4.4 Start motor at 30% speed. Monitor vibration — should be below 2.5 mm/s.
   4.5 Run at 30% for 30 minutes. Check bearing temperature — should be below 65°C.
   4.6 If all checks pass, increase to full speed. Log completion in maintenance system.

4. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
   - Vibration at full speed: < 3.0 mm/s
   - Bearing temperature at steady state: < 80°C
   - No audible abnormal noise

5. ESCALATION
   If vibration remains > 4.0 mm/s after correct installation, escalate to
   Sr. Maintenance Engineer immediately. Do not operate at full load.

6. DOCUMENTATION
   Record completion in SAP PM, update equipment history card, file PTW.
