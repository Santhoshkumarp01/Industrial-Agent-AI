STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Motor Insulation Check and Winding Inspection

SOP No: MT-EL-006 | Rev: 2 | Date: 2024-01-08
Equipment: BF Blower #1 Motor (ABB M3BP 160kW)
Estimated Duration: 3–4 hours

1. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
   1.1 Obtain Electrical Work Permit before starting.
   1.2 Perform LOTO on motor supply breaker. Test for dead using approved voltage tester.
   1.3 Discharge motor capacitors if present (wait 5 minutes after isolation).
   1.4 Only qualified electricians (Competent Person certified) may perform this work.
   1.5 Use insulated tools and insulating gloves rated for 1000V minimum.
   1.6 Ensure megger/insulation tester is calibrated (check sticker).

2. TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT REQUIRED
   - Digital megger (insulation resistance tester), 1000V DC rating
   - Digital multimeter (DMM) with temperature probe
   - Inspection lamp or flashlight
   - Thermal imaging camera (optional but recommended)
   - Cleaning materials: vacuum cleaner, compressed air, brush
   - Terminal box gasket (replacement if damaged)
   - Electrical contact cleaner spray
   - Torque screwdriver (0.5–3 Nm range)

3. INSPECTION FREQUENCY
   - Visual inspection: Weekly (during operation)
   - Megger test: Monthly (during planned shutdown)
   - Detailed internal inspection: Every 6 months
   - Winding resistance measurement: Every 3 months

4. PROCEDURE

   Step 1 — Pre-Inspection External Check (Motor Running)
   1.1 Observe motor for abnormal noise (bearing rumble, rotor rub, fan imbalance).
   1.2 Check motor vibration with handheld analyzer — should be <2.8 mm/s RMS.
   1.3 Check motor surface temperature with thermal camera:
       - Normal: 60–80°C (frame surface at rated load)
       - Caution: 80–100°C (check loading, cooling, ambient)
       - Critical: >100°C (shutdown immediately, investigate)
   1.4 Inspect cooling fan for damage, debris, or blockage.
   1.5 Check cable entry glands for moisture, dust ingress, or damage.

   Step 2 — Motor Isolation and Safety Verification
   2.1 Coordinate with operations for planned shutdown.
   2.2 Isolate motor at supply breaker. Apply LOTO padlock and tag.
   2.3 Test for dead at motor terminal box using approved voltage tester:
       - Test between all three phases (U-V, V-W, W-U)
       - Test each phase to earth (U-E, V-E, W-E)
       - Confirm zero voltage on all tests
   2.4 Short all three phases together temporarily using earthing stick.
   2.5 Display "Work in Progress" sign at motor and control panel.

   Step 3 — Terminal Box Inspection
   3.1 Remove terminal box cover screws (typically 6 × M6 screws).
   3.2 Inspect terminal box interior:
       - Moisture presence: Check gasket seal, cable gland seals, breather condition
       - Dust/dirt buildup: Clean with vacuum and dry compressed air
       - Corrosion on terminals: Clean with contact cleaner, replace if severe
       - Insect/rodent ingress: Clean, inspect cable insulation for damage
   3.3 Inspect cable terminations:
       - Check for discoloration (sign of overheating due to loose connection)
       - Check terminal tightness (torque to 2.5 Nm for M6 terminals)
       - Check cable insulation for cracks, burns, or degradation
   3.4 Photograph terminal condition for records.

   Step 4 — Insulation Resistance Test (Megger Test)
   4.1 Disconnect motor cable from supply terminals. Isolate motor completely.
   4.2 Remove motor winding star point links if accessible (to test phase-to-phase).
   4.3 Set megger to 1000V DC range.
   4.4 Measure insulation resistance for each winding to earth:
       - Connect megger: one probe to phase terminal, other to motor frame earth
       - Apply voltage for 60 seconds, note final stabilized reading
       - Repeat for all three phases: U-E, V-E, W-E
   4.5 Measure insulation resistance between phases (if star point disconnected):
       - Test U-V, V-W, W-U
   4.6 Record results and interpret:
       - GOOD: >100 MΩ (motor insulation in excellent condition)
       - ACCEPTABLE: 10–100 MΩ (monitor trend, retest monthly)
       - MARGINAL: 1–10 MΩ (plan for motor rewind or drying out)
       - FAIL: <1 MΩ (do not energize motor — immediate action required)
   4.7 Calculate Polarization Index (PI) if marginal results:
       - Take reading at 1 minute and 10 minutes
       - PI = R(10 min) / R(1 min)
       - PI >2.0: Acceptable
       - PI <2.0: Insulation degraded or contaminated — investigate

   Step 5 — Winding Resistance Measurement
   5.1 Use DMM in resistance (Ω) mode, 4-wire measurement if available.
   5.2 Measure DC resistance of each phase winding: U, V, W.
   5.3 Compare readings:
       - All three phases should be within ±5% of each other
       - Typical value for 160kW motor: 0.05–0.15 Ω per phase
       - Imbalance >10%: Possible turn-to-turn short — escalate to motor specialist
   5.4 Record winding temperature during test (affects resistance — use for trending).

   Step 6 — Internal Visual Inspection (if cover removable)
   6.1 Remove motor end covers (requires mechanical clearance — check drawings).
   6.2 Inspect stator windings:
       - Look for winding insulation cracks, discoloration, or burn marks
       - Check for loose winding wedges or coil movement
       - Check for moisture, oil, or dust contamination
       - Check winding end connections for tightness
   6.3 Inspect rotor:
       - Check rotor bars for cracks (especially in die-cast aluminum rotors)
       - Check for rotor rub marks on stator bore (clearance should be uniform)
       - Check for rotor balance weights (should be secure)
   6.4 Inspect bearings:
       - Check for noise by rotating shaft slowly by hand
       - Check for grease leakage, contamination, discoloration
       - Check bearing axial and radial play (should be minimal, <0.2mm)
   6.5 Clean motor interior with vacuum and dry compressed air (max 3 bar pressure).
   6.6 Reassemble end covers with new gaskets if originals are damaged.

   Step 7 — Reassembly and Energization Test
   7.1 Reconnect motor cable to terminal box.
   7.2 Torque terminal connections to 2.5 Nm.
   7.3 Reinstall terminal box cover with gasket. Torque screws to 1.5 Nm.
   7.4 Remove earthing stick and LOTO.
   7.5 Energize motor and run at no load for 5 minutes:
       - Listen for abnormal noise
       - Check vibration — should be <2.0 mm/s at no load
       - Check motor current — should be 20–30% of rated (32–48A for 160kW motor)
   7.6 Load motor to full load. Monitor for 30 minutes:
       - Current: Should be within ±10% of rated current (160A for 160kW at 400V)
       - Temperature: Should stabilize at <80°C frame temperature
       - Vibration: <2.8 mm/s RMS
   7.7 If all parameters acceptable, return to normal operation.

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
   - Insulation resistance (phase to earth): >100 MΩ at 1000V DC
   - Winding resistance balance: All phases within ±5%
   - No visible winding damage, moisture, or contamination
   - Vibration at full load: <2.8 mm/s RMS
   - Frame temperature at full load: <80°C
   - Current draw at full load: Within ±10% of rated

6. TROUBLESHOOTING
   - LOW INSULATION RESISTANCE (<1 MΩ):
     * Possible causes: Moisture, winding contamination, insulation degradation
     * Action: Dry out motor in oven at 80°C for 12 hours, retest
     * If still low: Plan motor rewind

   - WINDING RESISTANCE IMBALANCE (>10%):
     * Possible cause: Turn-to-turn short, loose connection, phase open circuit
     * Action: Escalate to motor specialist, do not energize

   - HIGH RUNNING TEMPERATURE (>100°C):
     * Check: Motor loading (should be ≤100% rated), cooling fan, ambient temp, voltage imbalance
     * Action: Reduce load, clean cooling passages, check supply voltage balance

   - HIGH VIBRATION (>4.5 mm/s):
     * Check: Bearing condition, rotor balance, coupling alignment, foundation bolts
     * Action: Bearing replacement, dynamic balancing, realignment

7. DOCUMENTATION
   Record in motor maintenance log: date, running hours, megger readings (all phases),
   winding resistance (all phases), observations, corrective actions.
   Plot insulation resistance trend over time to detect degradation early.
