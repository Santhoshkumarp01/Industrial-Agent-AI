STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Lubrication Circuit Inspection and Oil Change

SOP No: MT-LU-002 | Rev: 2 | Date: 2024-01-10
Equipment: Rolling Mill #1, Rolling Mill #3, BF Blower #1
Estimated Duration: 2–3 hours

1. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
   1.1 Equipment must be isolated (LOTO applied) before starting work.
   1.2 Oil can be hot (up to 70°C) — wear heat-resistant gloves.
   1.3 Avoid skin contact with used oil. Use nitrile gloves.
   1.4 Have spill kit and absorbent pads ready at work site.
   1.5 Dispose of used oil per environmental regulations (designated waste oil drums).

2. TOOLS AND MATERIALS REQUIRED
   - Oil drain pump (if gravity drain not available)
   - Oil filter wrench
   - Torque wrench (30–80 Nm range)
   - Oil analysis sample bottles (clean, labeled)
   - Replacement oil filter: OF-112 (RM1/RM3) or OF-115 (BF1)
   - Fresh lubrication oil: ISO VG 220 mineral oil, 20L minimum
   - Funnel with fine mesh filter
   - Cleaning rags and degreaser

3. INSPECTION FREQUENCY
   - Visual inspection: Weekly
   - Oil analysis sampling: Monthly
   - Oil filter replacement: Every 3 months or per condition monitoring
   - Complete oil change: Every 12 months or if contamination detected

4. PROCEDURE

   Step 1 — Pre-Inspection Visual Check
   1.1 Check oil level in sight glass — should be between MIN and MAX marks.
   1.2 Observe oil color through sight glass:
       - Clear amber: OK
       - Dark brown/black: Oxidation — oil change needed
       - Milky/cloudy: Water contamination — investigate source immediately
   1.3 Check for external leaks around seals, fittings, and pipe joints.
   1.4 Listen for unusual pump noise (cavitation, air entrainment).

   Step 2 — Oil Sampling for Analysis
   2.1 Run equipment at normal operating temperature for 30 minutes before sampling.
   2.2 Take sample from mid-point sampling valve (not from drain or top-up port).
   2.3 Flush sampling valve for 5 seconds before collecting sample.
   2.4 Fill sample bottle to shoulder line, seal immediately.
   2.5 Label: Equipment ID, date, running hours, technician name.
   2.6 Send to lab within 24 hours. Request: viscosity, particle count, water content, TAN.

   Step 3 — Oil Filter Replacement
   3.1 Shut down equipment. Ensure system is depressurized.
   3.2 Place drip tray under filter housing.
   3.3 Use filter wrench to remove filter element (turn counter-clockwise).
   3.4 Inspect old filter element:
       - Heavy metallic debris: Bearing wear — escalate immediately
       - Sludge buildup: Oil degradation — increase monitoring
       - Normal dirt: Acceptable
   3.5 Clean filter housing with lint-free rag.
   3.6 Lubricate new filter O-ring with clean oil.
   3.7 Install new filter element. Torque to 50 Nm (hand-tight + 1/2 turn).
   3.8 Fill housing with clean oil before sealing to avoid dry start.

   Step 4 — Complete Oil Change (if required)
   4.1 Shut down equipment. Ensure system is cool (<40°C) and depressurized.
   4.2 Place oil drain pan (minimum 25L capacity) under drain plug.
   4.3 Remove drain plug slowly. Allow complete drainage (minimum 20 minutes).
   4.4 Inspect drained oil for metal particles, water, or sludge.
   4.5 Clean drain plug magnetic tip. Re-install with new crush washer. Torque to 40 Nm.
   4.6 Replace oil filter (Step 3).
   4.7 Fill system with fresh oil through top-up port using funnel with mesh filter.
   4.8 Fill to MAX mark on sight glass. Do not overfill.
   4.9 Replace top-up cap. Check seal is intact.

   Step 5 — System Restart and Verification
   5.1 Remove LOTO. Start equipment at no load.
   5.2 Run for 5 minutes. Check for leaks around filter and drain plug.
   5.3 Stop equipment. Wait 10 minutes for oil to settle.
   5.4 Recheck oil level in sight glass. Top up if needed to MAX mark.
   5.5 Run equipment at normal load. Monitor oil pressure gauge:
       - Normal: 3.5–5.0 bar
       - Low (<3.0 bar): Stop and investigate
   5.6 Check oil temperature after 30 minutes — should stabilize at 60–75°C.

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
   - Oil level between MIN and MAX marks
   - Oil color: Clear amber (not dark or milky)
   - Oil pressure: 3.5–5.0 bar at normal operating temperature
   - No external leaks
   - Oil temperature: 60–75°C at steady state

6. TROUBLESHOOTING
   - LOW PRESSURE: Check filter blockage, pump wear, oil level, viscosity grade
   - HIGH TEMPERATURE: Check cooler cleanliness, ambient temp, oil contamination
   - WATER IN OIL: Inspect cooling water pipes, seals, condensation in breather
   - METALLIC PARTICLES: Bearing wear — escalate to engineering immediately

7. DOCUMENTATION
   Record in lubrication log: date, running hours, oil level, filter change, observations.
   Attach oil analysis report when received.
