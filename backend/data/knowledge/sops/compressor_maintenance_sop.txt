STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Compressor Valve Inspection and Intercooler Cleaning

SOP No: MT-COMP-008 | Rev: 2 | Date: 2024-01-12
Equipment: Compressor A (Reciprocating Air Compressor)
Estimated Duration: 6–8 hours

1. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
   1.1 Obtain Permit to Work (PTW) from operations supervisor.
   1.2 Perform LOTO on compressor motor supply and pneumatic isolation valves.
   1.3 Depressurize all receiver tanks and intercooler stages completely (vent to atmosphere).
   1.4 Verify zero pressure using calibrated pressure gauge before opening any covers.
   1.5 Wear safety glasses, hearing protection, and steel-toed boots.
   1.6 Compressed air can be hot (up to 120°C) — allow cooling to <40°C before starting work.
   1.7 Use proper lifting equipment for valve assemblies (15–20 kg weight).

2. TOOLS AND MATERIALS REQUIRED
   - Valve spring compressor tool (compressor-specific)
   - Torque wrench (20–120 Nm range)
   - Feeler gauge set (0.05–1.0 mm)
   - High-pressure water jet or chemical cleaning system
   - Replacement valve seats (VALVE-SEAT-A) if required
   - Valve springs (have spares available)
   - Gasket set (GASKET-SET-COMP)
   - O-ring set (SEAL-VITON-32)
   - Valve grinding compound (fine grade)
   - Cleaning solvent (non-flammable)
   - Compressed air supply (dry, oil-free)
   - Leak detection solution (soapy water)

3. MAINTENANCE FREQUENCY
   - Visual inspection during operation: Weekly
   - Valve inspection and cleaning: Every 3 months or 2,000 running hours
   - Valve replacement: Every 10,000 hours or when leakage detected
   - Intercooler cleaning: Every 6 months or when temperature rise >15°C above normal

4. PROCEDURE

   PART A — VALVE INSPECTION AND REPLACEMENT

   Step 1 — Compressor Shutdown and Depressurization
   1.1 Coordinate with operations for planned shutdown.
   1.2 Stop compressor. Close discharge isolation valve.
   1.3 Open drain valves on all receiver tanks and intercoolers. Vent to atmosphere.
   1.4 Apply LOTO on motor supply breaker and pneumatic isolation valves.
   1.5 Attach pressure gauge to cylinder head test port. Verify 0 bar pressure.
   1.6 Allow compressor to cool to ambient temperature (<40°C). Minimum 2 hours.

   Step 2 — Cylinder Head Removal
   2.1 Disconnect cooling water inlet/outlet hoses from cylinder head (if water-cooled).
   2.2 Remove cylinder head cover bolts in cross pattern (8 × M16 bolts, record torque).
   2.3 Lift cylinder head cover carefully using lifting eyes. Weight: ~25 kg.
   2.4 Inspect cylinder head gasket. Replace if damaged, compressed, or leaking.

   Step 3 — Suction Valve Inspection
   3.1 Locate suction valve assembly (typically on top of piston, accessed from head).
   3.2 Use valve spring compressor tool to compress valve springs.
   3.3 Remove valve retaining clip or snap ring.
   3.4 Lift out valve assembly (valve plate + springs + retainer).
   3.5 Inspect valve plate and seat:
       - Check valve plate flatness using straight edge and feeler gauge
       - Warpage >0.05mm: Replace valve plate
       - Check valve seat for pitting, erosion, carbon buildup
       - Measure valve spring free length:
         * New spring: 45mm typical
         * Replace if compressed to <40mm (loss of sealing force)
   3.6 Clean valve plate and seat with solvent. Remove carbon deposits carefully.
   3.7 If seat is damaged (pitting >0.3mm depth):
       - Replace valve seat VALVE-SEAT-A
       - Lap new seat with valve grinding compound
       - Test seal by applying light oil and checking for leakage when valve plate is held closed

   Step 4 — Discharge Valve Inspection
   4.1 Locate discharge valve assembly (typically in cylinder head or discharge passage).
   4.2 Remove discharge valve cover plate (4 × M10 bolts).
   4.3 Remove valve springs using spring compressor.
   4.4 Lift out discharge valve assembly.
   4.5 Inspect valve plate and seat (same criteria as suction valve in Step 3.5).
   4.6 Discharge valves experience higher temperature and pressure:
       - Check for heat discoloration (blue tint indicates overheating)
       - Check for valve plate warping or cracking
       - Replace if any cracks visible (can cause catastrophic failure)
   4.7 Clean and replace as needed.

   Step 5 — Valve Reassembly
   5.1 Install new valve seat if old one was removed. Torque to 60 Nm.
   5.2 Apply thin layer of valve grinding compound to valve seat.
   5.3 Place valve plate on seat. Rotate gently 10–15 times to lap surfaces together.
   5.4 Clean off all grinding compound with solvent. Surfaces should be mirror-smooth.
   5.5 Install valve springs. Ensure correct orientation (tighter coil pitch at bottom).
   5.6 Compress springs with tool. Install retaining clip or snap ring.
   5.7 Verify valve moves freely by pressing valve plate — should snap back when released.
   5.8 Install new gasket on cylinder head mating surface.
   5.9 Refit cylinder head cover. Torque bolts to 85 Nm in cross pattern (gradually tighten in 3 stages: 30, 60, 85 Nm).

   PART B — INTERCOOLER CLEANING

   Step 6 — Intercooler Preparation
   6.1 Locate intercooler heat exchanger (between compression stages).
   6.2 Close intercooler inlet and outlet isolation valves.
   6.3 Open intercooler drain valve. Drain condensate water completely.
   6.4 Disconnect intercooler from piping (may require removing flanges).

   Step 7 — Intercooler Tube Cleaning
   7.1 Inspect intercooler tubes for fouling, scale buildup, oil contamination.
   7.2 Cleaning method selection based on fouling type:
       - Light dust/dirt: Compressed air blow-through (max 6 bar pressure)
       - Oil contamination: Chemical cleaning with degreaser, flush with water
       - Hard scale: High-pressure water jet (100–150 bar) OR chemical descaling
   7.3 For chemical cleaning:
       - Fill intercooler with descaling solution (citric acid 5% solution)
       - Soak for 2 hours
       - Flush with clean water until pH neutral
   7.4 For water jet cleaning:
       - Insert water jet lance into each tube
       - Flush until water runs clear
       - Use tube cleaning brushes for stubborn deposits
   7.5 After cleaning, blow dry with compressed air.
   7.6 Inspect tubes with inspection light — should see clear passage through all tubes.

   Step 8 — Intercooler Pressure Test
   8.1 Reconnect intercooler to piping with new gaskets.
   8.2 Close drain valve. Open inlet/outlet valves.
   8.3 Pressurize intercooler slowly to working pressure (typically 10 bar).
   8.4 Hold pressure for 10 minutes. Check for leaks using leak detection solution.
   8.5 Acceptable leakage: Zero. Any leak = re-torque flanges or replace gaskets.

   Step 9 — Compressor Restart and Functional Test
   9.1 Remove LOTO after verifying all covers are installed and tools are removed.
   9.2 Start compressor in unloaded mode (suction valve held open).
   9.3 Run for 5 minutes. Listen for abnormal valve noise (chattering, metallic impacts).
   9.4 Check cylinder head temperature — should be <90°C after 5 minutes.
   9.5 Switch to loaded mode (normal operation).
   9.6 Monitor performance:
       - Discharge pressure: Should reach rated pressure (e.g., 10 bar) in <2 minutes
       - Intercooler outlet temperature: Should be <50°C (cooling effect restored)
       - Suction/discharge valve noise: Should be quiet, rhythmic clicking only
       - Motor current: Should be within ±5% of rated (e.g., 32A for typical 22kW compressor)
   9.7 Run for 1 hour at full load. Check:
       - Final stage discharge temperature: Should be <120°C
       - Intercooler pressure drop: Should be <0.5 bar (clean intercooler)
       - No air leaks from cylinder head gasket or valve covers
   9.8 If all parameters acceptable, return to normal operation.

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
   - Valve seat leakage: Zero (tested with leak detection solution at 10 bar)
   - Valve spring free length: >40mm (replace if shorter)
   - Cylinder head gasket: No leaks (soap test at working pressure)
   - Intercooler outlet temperature: <50°C at rated load
   - Intercooler pressure drop: <0.5 bar
   - Discharge pressure: Reaches rated pressure (10 bar) within 2 minutes
   - Final stage temperature: <120°C at full load
   - Motor current: Within ±5% of rated current

6. TROUBLESHOOTING
   - LOW DISCHARGE PRESSURE (<8 bar when rated is 10 bar):
     * Cause: Valve leakage (suction or discharge), piston ring wear, intercooler leakage
     * Action: Leak test valves individually, check piston rings, pressure test intercooler

   - HIGH DISCHARGE TEMPERATURE (>140°C):
     * Cause: Fouled intercooler, inadequate cooling, valve leakage, over-compression
     * Action: Clean intercooler, check cooling water flow, inspect valves

   - VALVE NOISE (loud metallic chattering):
     * Cause: Broken valve spring, valve seat erosion, valve plate cracked, foreign object
     * Action: Stop immediately. Inspect and replace damaged components.

   - INTERCOOLER DIFFERENTIAL PRESSURE HIGH (>1.0 bar):
     * Cause: Tubes blocked, scale buildup, internal corrosion
     * Action: Chemical cleaning or tube replacement

   - OIL CARRYOVER TO DISCHARGE (oily air output):
     * Cause: Worn piston rings, valve stem seals damaged, oil level too high
     * Action: Replace piston rings, valve seals, check oil level

7. DOCUMENTATION
   Record in compressor maintenance log: date, running hours, valve condition (suction/discharge),
   spring measurements, intercooler cleaning method, pressure test results, performance after restart.
   Update valve replacement schedule based on wear rate observed.
